About The Shift

Founded in 2009, The Shift Chiropractic was born from a simple mission: to revolutionize pain management in Oakland by combining traditional chiropractic wisdom with cutting-edge modern techniques. Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and approach to chiropractic care.

How long does it take to see results?

Most patients experience some relief within the first few sessions, often within 24-48 hours. However, the timeline varies depending on your condition's severity, how long you've had the issue, and how consistently you follow our treatment plan. Acute injuries may resolve in 2-4 weeks, while chronic conditions might require 8-12 weeks of regular treatment combined with home exercises. We'll provide a personalized timeline during your initial consultation based on your specific situation.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept most major insurance plans. We work directly with your insurance provider to maximize your benefits and minimize out-of-pocket costs. Our billing team will verify your coverage before your first appointment and explain your benefits clearly. We also offer flexible payment plans for those without insurance or with high deductibles. Contact us at theshiftchiropractic@gmail.com to discuss your specific insurance situation.

Is chiropractic care safe?

Absolutely. Chiropractic care is one of the safest healthcare practices when performed by licensed, trained professionals. Our team is fully licensed and certified with years of continuing education. We perform thorough assessments and may use imaging studies to rule out any contraindications. Our techniques are gentle and evidence-based, designed to minimize risk while maximizing therapeutic benefit. Serious complications are extremely rare, and we prioritize your safety above all else.

What should I expect during my first visit?

Your first visit typically lasts 60-90 minutes. We'll start with a comprehensive consultation covering your health history, current symptoms, lifestyle, and goals. Then we'll perform a thorough physical examination, including spinal assessment and posture analysis. We may take X-rays or other imaging if needed. Based on our findings, we'll explain your condition in detail and discuss treatment options. Most patients receive their first treatment during this visit and leave with clear expectations about their care plan and timeline.

Can I continue my regular activities during treatment?

In most cases, yes. We encourage you to stay active as it promotes healing and prevents stiffness. However, we may recommend modifications to certain activities during your acute phase of treatment. Our personalized treatment plan includes specific guidance about what activities are safe and which ones to temporarily avoid. We'll also teach you proper body mechanics and ergonomics to prevent re-injury. As you progress, we gradually return you to your full activity level. We work with athletes and active individuals regularly to maintain their performance while healing.
